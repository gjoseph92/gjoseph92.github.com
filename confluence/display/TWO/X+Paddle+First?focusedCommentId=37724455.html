    THIS REVIEW IS WRITTEN BY JESS MADDING, not jake
    You are so lucky to be doing a hybrid trip because it is AWESOME. That being said, Zach and I didn't do the trip that we had planned (thanks hurricane) so I 'll give you our daily and some general advice.

    Day 1: Easy. We recommend pairing your freshman so they get to know eachother, same with your co. Plenty of campsites, trees are a little thin so it's hard to bear bag well per usual. We did get lost on our way to the drop off but asked for directions in town. Drop of is literally like a patch of gravel in the trees next to a road.

    Day/night 2: Storm hits! We made it about a mile over the course of a couple hours before camping out on the beach until 6 and then canoeing to the evac point, where we set up camp at midnight and promptly fell asleep. On the beach a decent shelter can be built by using your canoes as sides but it takes a bit of engineering. Although we were supposed to be evacuated to our next point by ssstaff, renee and justine later deemed the evac unusable. I'm not sure why but keep that in mind! See below on night canoeing.

    Day 3: Up at dawn to meet the other X. Beautiful morning canoe. It's not super confusing to find the meet point, just pay attention to the outlets and stay close to shore. And don't pass the tiny little island near round barn. We also never found bigalow loj but did end up camping at this site.

    Day 4: Instead of hiking the ridge since we were a day behind, we hiked up to where we were suppose to stay night 3, met ejo and austin (which they certainly were not expecting), got water, and then continued on with them towards the camp site on the other side of little bigelow mountain. The hike up to a 20 was rather strenuous but along the ridge is not as easy as it looks. We misjudged, thinking there was little elevation change and only a mile, but this is not true and the end at least is a lot of downhill. We skipped lunch and did solos instead. The views are AMAZING though.

    Day 5: Easy as pie to the road.

    General Advice
    1. Canoeing is easy but it's not effortless and becomes damn near impossible in bad weather/high winds. If conditions start to worsen, immediately head for shore so you at least have the option of waiting it out on the beach. Zach and I got caught off shore in a whirlpool of sorts (they're had been whitecaps for about twenty minutes) since we were the last boat and struggled to shore for half an hour, our freshman already on the beach laughing at us. Conditions on the water change quickly!

    2. Night Canoeing: incredibly dangerous, just don't do it, undertaken only after we talked to the coordinators. You are on a lake and there are infinity beaches to camp on, if you can't make it to a campsite before a decent hour, don't even try and pick a destination on the map. We navigated via the outline of land masses based on their elevation which works well but we did end up in an unforseen sandbar. This is why you should canoe in the middle of the lake near all those big islands- Stay to the LEFT.

    3. We bear-bagged at our first and last campsites but not when we slept on the beach and just hid our food under piles of sand and the canoe. Although we had no problems, by now you should realize we aren't the model leaders. However, putting safety first is paramount at all times and if you end up back at Tufts on time with no injuries, that's all that matters. Doesn't matter how you get there and fun will be had along the way.

    4. Canoeing first is AWESOME...which we realized even though we originally wanted to hike first! You end up with lighter packs and bonded freshman and can dump whatever you don't want on ssstaff. Just be sure not to LEAVE STUFF IN THE CANOES when you switch-we lost forgot our spice kit, a pot, and a stove. oops.

    5. GEAR: You NEED sunglasses and rainpants, if it rains you will get more wet canoeing than if you were backpacking.

    6. We drank purified lake water with no problem.

    7. Cell Phone Service: We made many calls (seriously, renee loved us). Service was attainable where we slept near the evac but NOT at round-barn, nor anywhere on the lake, so we had to drive out to the road at round barn.

    I hope all of this didn't scare you because this trip is REALLY WONDERFUL and our canoeing was the hardest part just because of the storm. Just make sure you have a thorough understanding of the map before heading out so you can adapt as need be.

    PLEASE email if you have questions or call me, this was mad long and confusing but it's because we basically replanned our trip every morning and evening based on conditions. jmadding8@gmail.com (650) 740 3734

    Alright future Hybrid fools, this review has been a long time coming.

    First things first: hybrid is THE SHIT. And don't let anybody tell you any different.
    The first minor snafu we ran into was in getting dropped off at the right place: just make sure it's clear with the canoe people whereabouts the boats are going to be left. Can't remember exactly what the mixup was, but I think it involved the state boat launch (being the right place? being the wrong place? sorry guys, my memory's failing me...).
    But, other than that, the first day went off without a hitch. Time permitting, it might be worthwhile to spend half an hour just paddling around in the cove where the boats are waiting, just to make sure your freshman have the hang of things and are able to canoe in a straight line/turn/execute perfect J-strokes.
    Night 1 campsite was pretty awesome, it's got a toilet plopped down right in the middle of the woods--no walls or anything, the perfect opportunity to encourage your kids to get in touch with the great outdoors while moving their bowels.
    Day 2 went smoothly as well, and the island campsite is the tits. Try yelling off the mountains and listening for the echo as a group! If the weather is good on Day 1 and 2, you'll have plenty of time to get to your campsites--we got in well before dark both nights, and had lots of time to fuck around and make dinner and what not. Of course this all depends on the weather and your kids, but for the most part the canoeing legs were pretty laid back and you should plan for some games and activities to kill time.
    Day 3: coordinate really really well with the other X group (and support staff if you're getting a hike-in) where you'll be meeting. The X1/X2 meetup went just fine, but we waited around for a couple hours trying to find support staff, who were there already but in a slightly different place. Cell phone service is also basically non-existent here. And give yourself time for the hike--not too tricky, but mostly uphill and the wee little freshmen who have been canoeing and not carrying any weight will be taken aback by the weight of their packs.
    Day 4: It's a pretty long schlep with lots of elevation change, but you get some incredible views of the lake you just paddled and surrounding mountains. Try to get camp broken down nice and early in the morning so you have time to enjoy the views and aren't rushed. We didn't have any troubles per se, but it was definitely a tough hike for our more inexperienced kids. There's also a swimmable lake at the campsite.
    Day 5: Woke up super early and still didn't make it to the bus on time. The hike is easy enough and mostly downhill though.

    Alrightyo, I think that's all I've got for now! Hit me up if you've got any questions, and have a motherfucking blast! Seriously, hybrid is the cream of the crop, you ain't never had it so good.

    J-Sav

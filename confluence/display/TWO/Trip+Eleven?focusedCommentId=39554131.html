    Amanda Jichlinski says:

    I did this trip with Mighty (Summer 2008) and we had a fantastic time. The three...

    I did this trip with Mighty (Summer 2008) and we had a fantastic time. The three peaks you summit all have impressive views and the hiking is beautiful (how could it not be?). We even saw a moose! Some things that I remember from the trip:

     -  Mascot Pond's campsite is a little tricky to find. Walk down the trail for the pond, you'll see the pond. Then keep walking down the grassy path for only a little bit and you'll see the campsite on your right. There is a legit campsite, so don't camp on the beach as some groups have done in the past. If it's a clear night, go out to the pond to look at the stars since everywhere else on your trip, the sky will be blocked by trees.

    -  Getting to the top of Cascade is steep with a lot of bouldering. If you're taking the trip the other way (starting from Success Pond Rd.) you'll have to pass packs on the way down. If this is your second day, be sure to have plenty of water cuz your freshies will drink a lot of theirs.

     - Trident Cole campsite is with tarps but they have a bear box and an outhouse to look forward to.

    - On Day 3, I HIGHLY suggest (and I know Mighty and all my freshmen back me up on this) that you get off the AT and take the Dryad Falls trail. Go check out the falls! You'll see one of the most amazing views on your whole trip and it doesn't add any time or distance. It's an amazing view, and incredibly fun. Be prepared for a steep hike to Gentian Pond campsite but nothing worse than you've already experienced. It's not too long and you can take it slow. Your freshmen will be fine and they'll have a great time at the falls. PLEASE go there. Too few groups do.

    - Gentain pond has shelters and platforms and is probably the nicest campsite with a great view. There's a bear box too. You'll like it there.

    - Not much to say about Day 4. Mt. Success has a great view, as expected. That night at the Carlo Col campsite, there is a good shelter and bear box. The water source is there, it's just a small creek so washing dishes and filling water bottles takes some time.

     - On the last day hiking out to Success Pond Road, keep your eye out for the blazes. Most of the time, the trail is clear but once you get onto the worn-out logging roads, it gets easy to miss the blazes. Towards the end, you go down an oldlogging road for a while, walking out in the open, but then have to cut back into the woods so look out for the marks.

    That's about it. Have a fantastic time!

    Jess and I did this trip 2010 and we also had a blast. -Finding your way on...

    Jess and I did this trip 2010 and we also had a blast.

    -Finding your way onto the trail the first day is very difficult. Talk to Jess before you leave

    -First night at Mascot Pond we slept on the beach because it was so nice out. There is a legit campsite though it wasn't particularly level, but we set up tarps in case we needed to come over in the middle of the night (which we didn't).

    -Agreeing with Amanda, TAKE THE DRYAD FALLS ROUTE. It is an absolutely amazing view and from what I heard from the group doing the reverse of our trip it is substantially easier. It is a pretty strenuous uphill hike only at the very end to actually get to Gentian Pond.

    - On the last day you hike out onto a big logging road that will likely be blocked to your left. If you walk up to the right about 100 yards you'll connect to a similar looking logging road that goes uphill to left and down a little to the right. This is not actually the pickup site. You have to keep walking to the right on the logging road for about another mile (its all flat, level and a little downhill so it shouldnt take too long). You'll pass the Goose Eye Trail about 6 tenths of a mile down the road on your right and keep walking until the road you are on dead ends with another bigger logging road. This is Success Pond road and there will be a big street sign on the right that says Success Pond Rd. and it also says something about Goose Eye Trail and Success Trail.

    -Our freshmen were able to handle all of the steeper parts of the trip without having to pass packs however there are some spots, mainly days 2 and 4, that could require you to pass packs.

    And I agree with everything Amanda says too.

    Day One
    We were dropped off at a gas station just up the road from the "trail head," or where you cross a bridge over a river to get onto logging roads. These roads are incredibly confusing, if you do this trip, I'll draw you a map of how to get to your trail head.
    There are blackberries on the first part of the trail, before you get to the actual trail! The first night, we chose not to drink the pond water. Instead, if you head back to the trail (the pond is on a little off shoot trial) there is water down a ravine to the left of the trail. It's about a 3-5 minute walk.

    Day Two
    We met ss staff on the top of mt. hayes (i think this is the mt). It's a big, open rocky area. The support staffers came up a trail a little further along the peak. Your trail for the rest of the day is down and to the left, you don't need to cross the whole peak. Also, there's a false peak before mt. hayes.

    Day 3
    We crossed the other group at dream lake (which is pretty nasty, but we swam anyways). There is another lake you will come across first, stay to the right of that lake. The trail gets a little confusing. also, DRYAD FALLS ARE INSANELY BEAUTIFUL. The trail might get slippery and mudding if it rains heavily, but seriously so worth it. They are a one minute walk off of the trail. I think taking this route is about the same as taking the other route, unsure of what benefits the other route has. Also, there are little streams along the way where you can get water. I don't believe these are on the map. Right before you get to the campsite, the trail opens up into an area where it is unmarked and you're near a swamp that should be on your right. Stay to the left and go uphill. You will think you have been going uphill for a million years and then the shelter pops out of nowhere.

    Day 4
    another great day. gets pretty steep at the end.

    Day 5
    See what Jake said above about the pickup and be sure to discuss this with the groups on your bus!

    THIS TRIP IS AWESOME

    Sooooooooo due to the hurricane we did not do to much of the trip, but the part we did was fun! but important thing: the way you reach the trailhead has changed! It seems like now it might be mildly less confusing. After drop off, you cross the bridge and take a left. Stay on that road; it curves around to the right (there might be a trail that forks to the left early on, which you don't take). There is a small rise and then a fork--take a left. Follow that road, and eventually you'll reach a bridge. Cross it. Immediately after, there is a trail to the right that you take into the woods. after a while, you will come out to a road, and you'll take a right. follow that for a while. Then the power anal sign will be on your right and you're golden. the trail goes off to the left. Throughout, there are some signs that say AMC Trail.  It's only .8 miles to your campsite.

    A couple of my freshmen got cut on the sharp rocks in mascot pond, so be careful. Don't drink the pond water; follow the trail back down to where there's a stream on your right (if you're going backwards).  The campsite is fine! It's right near the beach, a little past it.

    it was cool though...do it!

    Orli T. Gottlieb says:

    2012 Leaders: Jordan Ko and Joshua Elliott Have bus drop you off at the big bri...

    2012 Leaders: Jordan Ko and Joshua Elliott

    Have bus drop you off at the big bridge on the east side of Route 16, just north of where it intersects Route 2. From there, follow the dotted red line on the diagram. ﻿
    This avoids having to drive the bus over dirt roads, and should save time overall.

    Mascot pond campsite does not exist anymore; it was likely destroyed during a hurricane. The pond itself is a great place to hang out, but we did not go in the water (icky). We arrived there pretty early and had plenty of time. We kept hiking from there and camped off the trail some distance later (before Mount Hayes).

     Day 2

    We summited Mount Hayes and continued to Trident Col. There is a false peak right before Mount Hayes, the actual summit is a big open area. We arrived there very early at Trident Col (the goal for the night) and decided to keep going to Page Pond. When we arrived at Page there were two people already set up, it was a beautiful spot but did not have enough room for them and us. We continued hiking to Dream Lake, and arrived close to dark. There was ample room and we had an easy time setting up camp and a campfire.

     Day 3

    We hiked a very short distance to Dryad Falls and met up with our cross trip (NH, Trip One) early in the day (since they had a long ways to go after). The falls are gorgeous and we frolicked for a very long time.  Only skip Dryad Falls if you hate puppies and sunshine. When we had tired of our fun, we continued to the Gentian Pond campsite. There was a lean-to and many tent platforms. We stayed on one of the platforms and had a large area to ourselves.

    Day 4

    We summited Mount Success. There were many false peaks leading up to the summit, but it was a very pretty view from there. There was a water source about half way through the day, though it might not have been there in a drier year. The final stretch before the campsite had some very rough terrain. Large boulders required coordination, but we did not have to pass packs. We were somewhat disheartened right before reaching the Carlo Col campsite, because we thought that we had to get over another hump before reaching it. We realized as soon as we started walking that we were right there. We then took the Carlo Col trail to the campsite. Day four had an awesome amount of moss to pet, and beautiful alpine terrain.

     Day 5

    We continued on the Carlo Col trail to Success Pond Road. It was an easy but very wet hike down. Near the bottom there was a nice spot where the path was next to a stream, after reaching the road we went back there and made a very cold Jacuzzi by moving some rocks. At the road there were also some blackberry bushes.

    We waited at the road for a very long time, and realized that out cross group had taken our bus. This was because they were waiting at the same location we had started, and our bus drove by there and they flagged it down. LOL just tell your cross group to double-check the bus they get on.

    Overall, amazing! Our Day 2 was the longest, distance-wise (as we hiked several miles further than the goal campsite), but it was worth it to have hours to play at Dryad Falls on Day 3. Everyone agreed Day 4 was the most challenging overall. Not too difficult of a trip, but definitely challenged our freshmen, who were a pretty fit group. Do this trip! It was a lot of fun!

    Lauren E. Godles says:

    I totally second what Sarah says... I would never do this trip the other way.&nb...

    I totally second what Sarah says... I would never do this trip the other way.  The best part was ending up at the Lodge on the last night. The stars were amazing, and then when we got cold we could go inside to sleep. There's also a big porch where our kids Iron Chef-ed it up while Gideon and I climbed up the ski lift and sat up there for an amazing view.

    Here's the play by play for how our trip went:

    Day One: This was the hardest day in a lot of ways. It took us six hours to drive to the drop off because we had to first drop off another Vermont group. We ate lunch on the bus to save us some time, and that was DEFINITELY a good call. Good news is that the instructions to get to the trail head were perfect, and we found it no problem. However, it is about four miles up-hill with all the gear you left with, which was a little rough. Especially since the kids are still tired/shy, we had to really keep the energy up. Also, to note, Skylight Pond is too still to be a water source, but there is a small running stream closer to the trail that you can use before getting to Emily Proctor. By the time we got to camp, it was already getting dark, and someone was using the shelter.  There are tent platforms a few hundred feet up where we built tarps, but it was hard to teach the kids while it was getting dark and we were trying to cook dinner AND build a fire. I would recommend introducing tarp knots on the first day at Tufts while you aren't doing anything to avoid this situation in the future. Once we did get a fire going though, we had a great night.

    Day Two: This day was longer but our packs were lighter, so that was a big help. We did some pair hiking since the trail was very easy to follow and there wasn't too much elevation.   Definitely our easiest day.  We stopped for lunch on a look out on Mt. Roosevelt for one of the best views of the trip. It was amazing, definitely stop there! Again, someone was using the shelter, but there was a nice open area to build tarps and plenty of space to bear bag.

    Day 3: Got woken up in the best hike-in ever from SS! They made us eggs and brought fruit and orange juice. We woke up super early but the breakfast really gave us a kick start, so we actually made it past Lincoln Gap before we had lunch. (Sadly, there's nowhere nice to eat at Lincoln Gap.) Last part of the hike was amazing- it actually looked like an enchanted forest (but really, it did). We stayed at Battell that night and got to use the beautiful shelter. You can definitely fit 10 in there! Also, we miscalculated that day... thought it was 7.4 but really it was like 6.4.

    Day 4: Longest day of hiking but also the best day. It's pretty much all peaks with not too much up and down in between. There was also a surprise water source after one of the peaks! There was a sign pointing to it... it's a few hundred feet off the trail. It's small but it helps with the water rationing.  We also pushed it really hard this day and didn't eat lunch until the "Orvis Lookout" at Glen Ellen. Not really much to see, but after lunch we had so little hiking left! The ski lodge was the best surprise ever, and the kids were so happy. Amazing night there.

    Day 5: Pretty steep getting out (need to climb down some ladders, etc), but the pick up spot was easy and the bus came within ten minutes of us being there.

    HAVE SO MUCH FUN let me know if you have any questions!!

    Lauren

    2012 Leaders: Arlen Weiner and Lake Jazarus

    General impression: amazing trip! There were so many great lookouts/views and it was

    not an incredibly difficult trip- it had its intense uphills but those were complimented by

    some relaxing flat areas, downhills, and great views. There were also shelters at every

    campsite. Here’s the breakdown by day:
     
    Day 1: The trailhead was exactly where the directions said and was super easy to find.

    We were the last drop-off this day so we got a pretty late start (about 2pm) but we made

    it to Emily Proctor by 6pm so we still had enough daylight to set up camp and cook. The

    hike was moderate with some steep uphills and some flats. Skylight Pond is too still for a

    water source, but we didn’t need to fill up yet. Bread Loaf Mountain is not really a peak

    but there is a nice lookout about 1/10 of a mile off the trail. There’s no sign for it, but

    the LT curves off to the right and the trail to the lookout keeps going straight. At Emily

    Proctor there was a small stream for water but we had to do some searching for a good

    place to fill up. There was also a shelter that we all squeezed in for the night. There is

    also a tenting area near the shelter if needed.
    Day 2: This was probably one of the easier days- we got to Cooley Glen by 2pm! The

    peaks are not really peaks so don’t be alarmed if you just suddenly start going down after

    going up for a bit. We also never saw a sign for Clark Brook Trail, which was our evac

    route, so that was a little concerning. Killington View was unbelievably beautiful and

    was a great place to take a break! Cooley Glen also had a nice shelter that we all fit in and

    had a good stream for water.
    Day 3: This was a great day! After a short uphill, we had a great view from Mt. Grant and

    then continued on our way to Sunset Ledge. There was a peak before Sunset Ledge that

    we thought was it but after continuing another quarter mile, we got to the actual Sunset

    Ledge and it was beautiful! After Lincoln Gap, the hike got more difficult- it was about

    2 miles straight uphill but definitely doable. Plus you end the uphill by arriving at camp

    so it’s rewarding. There was a great caretaker at Battell named Tim who looked up the

    weather for us and was just generally good company. The water source there was ok but

    not great. However, there was a water source about 1/10 of a mile before the campsite

    that was a little better.
    Day 4: This day probably had the best views. We began by continuing the uphill from

    the day before up to Mt. Abraham, which was difficult but doable. This was by far our

    best peak! It was beautiful! Lincoln Peak, Nancy Hanks Peak, and Mt. Ellen were all at

    the tops of a chairlift and had some nice views, but weren’t really peaks. There was also

    a surprise water source after Nancy Hanks Peak (there was a sign on the trail for water

    and it’s about 100 ft off the trail). We didn’t actually get to go to Glen Ellen or the water

    source there because of construction. We just continued on to Stark’s Nest, which was

    an enclosed lodge at the top of a ski lift. It was incredible! Especially because this was

    the night it was raining. Apparently there is an incredible view from there in which you

    can see all the way to Mt. Washington, but it was so foggy we couldn’t even see 10 feet

    in front of us. Stark’s Nest also had a rain barrel as a water source which was so much
    cleaner and more convenient than a stream.
    Day 5: This was a short day but very steep downhill. There were some parts with

    metal bars/ladders to climb down and since it was wet it took us a little longer. But no

    problems. Our bus was waiting for us at the bottom when we arrived! Also you can stop

    at the Ben and Jerry’s factory on the way back!!

    --Arlen and Jake

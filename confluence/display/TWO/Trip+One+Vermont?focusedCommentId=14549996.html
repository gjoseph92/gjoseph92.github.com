    This trip is a great moderate trip.  There's a lot of elevation gain the first day but it's very manageable.  Starks Nest is great except for the water situation.  We tried to plan ahead and pack extra water but still needed to fill up so that we had enough for the next day.  We had to walk all the way down a black diamond ski slope to a tiny creek on the left...and then back up.  It's not pleasant, but at least it's there.  The second day we had lunch at Castle Rock but actually went all the way to Battel that day.  Battel was nice and it made for a better split in mileage.  We had a combined hike-in with our reverse group at the parking lot on day three, which worked pretty well.  Cooley Glen was our third night and Skyline Lodge was the fourth.  We actually stayed at the tent site near Skyline, which was really great.  There's a small "Sunset Ledge" nearby that did indeed have a great sunset.  The last day is short and sweet.  No issues with the pick-up.

      Agreed with everything LVT said. Day 1 water situation is really bad. Unless you hike in extra water you will need to walk down the ski slope which sucks. There aren't too many epic views but there is usually at least one or two each day. Starks Nest has an awesome view and DON'T miss Sunset Ledge at sunset.

    LINC & TRISH: What a great trip!!  A17 had a fantastic time on this route.  It was not too challenging some nice climbs and peaks (mostly ridge hikes) and diverse terrain including the stunning boreal forest that felt like Narnia. We had some tough times with lack of a water source between Cooley Glen and Emily Proctor. Bear bagging was not possible at any of these shelters, but apparently not necessary either because there are no bears (according to caretakers). Instead, mice need to be averted, so we needed to know how to make a critter line. Luckily, many of the huts had little strings where you could hang small bear bag like things. We would recommend future trip leaders know how to amke a critter line just in case! There were shelters at every location, and the skyline ledge was absolutely beautiful - we recommend snagging spots there if ya can.  P.S. no swimming so frosh don't need bsuits.  Overall, beautiful, momentous, sweaty.

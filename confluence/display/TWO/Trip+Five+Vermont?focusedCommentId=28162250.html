    Incredible trip! Swimming every night but the first night (two gorgeous lakes and one river), and stunning views from the day 2 and 3 peaks. Shelters every night, though we only used one once when the weather was horrible. The hike out is a little confusing. You end up cutting through someone's back yard. Just make a right on the first road you hit (after walking down that person's driveway), and then a left 200 or so meters down the road to cross a bridge up to Rt 7. Another confusing point was on day four when the LT comes out onto USFS Rt. 10. You have to follow the road a bit down the hill past a parking lot where you pick up the trail again on the right. The entrance to the Green Mountain Trail is just past that if you're interested in using that route to Little Rock Pond instead of the LT/AT. Overall great trip! I definitely recommend it.

    From Alex Freedman, TWO 2010

    The trip I did with Sarah was awesome (the Vaj trail). We did it South to North, which I especially liked. I would say it's a medium to medium-hard trip, but the lengths of days have a little flexibility, because of hut placement, so it can be tweaked depending on group skill. After our longest day (day two), we pushed our kids up a mountain for sunset, which was spectacular. And the last full day was our shortest, next to a lake so we could chill and swim and enjoy. There aren't a lot of super summits (a few great veiws, though), but it's on the AT, so you get awesome through-hikers; there is a fair level of tree cover to make hiking more pleasant; the terrain ramp up nicely; there are shelters and/or platforms at all sites; and there is a river in the middle and an awesome lake at the last site, which we used as a chill day and for our hike-in (which was easily located). The last morning hike-out was also very short, which is always nice.

    2012 Leaders: Alexa Petersen & Fez

    Our trip was STUPENDOUS. It was a stupendously easy-to-medium difficulty trip in beautiful parts of Vermont. Watch out for gremlins, hyperboles, and rogue trail heads. BEWARE OF THE SQUIRRELS. This trip is good for freshmen who are beginners or have never hiked before. Nothing like killer squirrels to get them started on the right foot. The first day is a very quick hike, and the campsite is nice. There is a small shelter and a few platforms. There's a rul weird trickle trickle water source, that takes a rul long time to fill up, so plan ahead for that. The second day is your STUPENDOUSLY long day. Be aware that this day is not particularly challenging in terms of elevation gain and loss, but it is a lot of ground to cover and your kids will probably be very tired. Important: When you get to the top of Bromley, your first peak, there are a number of trails to go down. LOOK FOR THE ONE WITH BLAZES (they will be white). The rest of the trails won't have blazes because they are ski trails usually wide enough to be roads. You make have to walk a minute or two down the trail to find the blazes, so give your kids a break on the top and have you two go look for the right trail. The map tells you that you will hike 3 peaks, but you only have look out points on the first 2 (you will reach the top of the third one under the tree line). There are 2 campsites near each other on the second night, we recommend picking the closer one to you (even though we originally planned to go to the farther one). Our kids were exhausted and it was a very nice shelter with a stupendous booming water source (and one additional platform). Day three: Be careful the boards are slippery. The squirrels are also slippery. Relatively stupendous day, with a great lookout on Bakers Peak (we met the other group for lunch on the peak). The campsite is a great place to swim, its by a nice river. You will see it right after you cross the suspension bridge. The shelter is small though with no other platforms, and the through hikers got there first. We ended up sleeping outside which they tell you not to do but it was stupendous. Great stars. Day 4: This was rul stupendous time. We told them they had a long day, and got to our campsite before lunch. We ate lunch by the water on a rock cliff and then surprised them that we were at their campsite!! They felt stupendously about this. SQUIRRELS FOR DINNER!! Thats what Iron Chef is for. The shelter at this campsite was more or less a Marriott Courtyard. Seriously. There were also platforms. The caretaker lives there too. Last day was an easy hike on a steady but mildly steep downhill. The elevation that TWO has written on their trips forms for this day is a lie--its a pretty sharp downhill for us and a pretty exhausting uphill on the first day for our opposite trip. Overall, we had a blast!!!

    A brief discussion on the perplexity of trail heads:
    The trail heads on this trip are a little tricky. We highly suggest having a smart phone on the bus with you, because it really helped us navigate. The drop off is quite easy: its right off the road on a small dirt parking lot and is well-marked. However, on the last day, when hiking towards the pick up point, you will come to a fork in the trail (which is pretty wide at this point). Take the trail marked with blue blazes (as is the entire hike out trail). The trail with the blue blazes will bring you right to the trailhead.The other option has no blazes. If you choose the option with no blazes, it will take you to someones backyard, which is about 1/4 mile from the actual trailhead. This is obviously not ideal, but either one will get you out.  IMPORTANT: in past TWO years, in a galaxy far far away, people are dropped off (our opposite trip) and picked up (our trip) at the trail head in someone's backyard. This is not the actual trail head, the actual trailhead is about 1/4 mile down the road, so you should talk to coordinators and/or bus drivers in advance.

    Love and other drugs,
    Fez and Alexa, TWO 2012

    This was a great trip. The day hikes were not very difficult and the day-hike at the end was the perfect challenge. A couple comments

    Notes on the Trip: 

    MAKE SURE YOUR FRESHMEN HAVE GOOD GEAR! We had many freshmen with horrible rain pants that shredded, fleeces that were heavy and didn't dry, etc. It is essential that they have a good pair of waterproof pants and raincoat as well as their fleece. It may seem obvious, but it gets overlooked a lot.

    Watch out for completely flooded trails. The flooded area can get quite deep and if you fall, you may find yourself completely soaked.

    You have no bear boxes at any campsite. Good luck bear bagging. Some places have better trees than others. Also, watch out for old tarp bags that will tear with a lot of food in them. We could use some new tarp bags.

    Make a fire if you need to. It will take a while and a lot of team work. In the rain, birch bark is heaven sent and try to tear appart rotten wood to find dryer bits inside.

    We made the hike all the way to the Horn. It was a great day hike. We left the campsite at 8:30 and we were back by around 5:30 so it's a full day, but absolutely great (weather permitting). The hike to the Horn from Saddleback is not too strenuous and doesn't add much. If you don't go all the way, pass the peak of Saddleback just a bit (3 minute walk) and you'll see a great view of the Horn. It's very windy up there so take hats, fleeces, and rain gear.

    Day 3 at, I believe, South Pond is a great place to meet Maine 2 which crosses you. There is a canoe at the pond and you can paddle about. Or just eat lunch naked.

    Notes on Campsites: 

    For the first campsite, try to take extra rope. The platform is old and sunken and pretty far from any good trees. It is hard to set up the tarps properly so that you don't get soaked if it rains. The shelter is nice but can get full of through hikers so don't rely on it.

    Second campsite has no shelter or platform. The area to the right of the fire-pit (facing the lake) makes a great place for tarps. Anrew created a system with one tarp above the other but tarp 1 drained into a created gutter and away from tarp 2. Really nice.

    Piazza Rock campsite: Good platforms next to trees are great for tarps and there is also a large shelter (fits about 12, packed).

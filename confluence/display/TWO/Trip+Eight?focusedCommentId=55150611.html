    This was A GREAT trip. We had pretty terrible/cold weather for most of hte trip, so we didnt get to do any swimming, but the ponds/falls were even awesome just to look at. We didnt even try to find the campsite on day 2, though everyone told us it was very hard to find, and just hiked on to the campsite for day 3 because of the rain, and slept with NH-4 there that night, which is a nice option to have.     This is a WONDERFUL trip! There are some challenging portions of the trail (the first mile in particular is pretty steep), but overall a good trip for beginners/inexperienced hikers. Nights 1, 3, and 4 have great campsites with latrines, but Zealand Falls is not a campsite!! You have to continue hiking past Zeacliff to find suitable camping spot. In the past, trips have camped at Zeacliff pond, but we camped at Zeacliff. This was not ideal, but our group was remarkably slow and we had great weather so weren't too worried about potential storms. Again, this was NOT an ideal spot to camp. Glassman and Hoff hiked all the way to Guyot campsite on day 2, but for slow groups that is a very long day. The official campsites also have plenty of other visitors. Platforms on nights 1 and 3!! To get to Guyot campsite you see the sign and walk down for about 15 or 20 minutes before you reach the campsite itself.

    Water spots to swim on days 1, 2, and 4. Day 4 is the longest day in terms of mileage, but it is almost entirely decline. We were able to get to the falls at a reasonable hour because we won't up early.

    The river/bridge issue on day 4 has been resolved and we had no issue figuring it out. There is a bridge you cross to get the the falls. On your way back you cross that bridge again, take a sharp right, and follow the trail until you get to a clearing where you can cross the river. The water was pretty low so we had no issues crossing, only mild chaos.

    Katie Lowe says:

    Day one:

    Kedron Flume Trail to Ethan Pond Trail, staying at Ethan Pond
    Drop off at the Willey House site-ish. We ate lunch here before starting up the trail. There is a small museum/ranger station there. Nice picnic area. Kedron Flume trail was fairly steep, but we didn't have any major issues. It is a harder first day than many. There are a few nice viewpoints to look across the notch, but is was mostly wooded. Stayed at the Ethan Pond Shelter. Signage was clear, and we found the campsite easily. It's on the opposite side of the pond from the trail, and you cross the outlet of the pond. There is a caretaker at the site and there are tent platforms and bear boxes. The water was a little janky (I used a pump). We easily arrived before dark.

    Day Two:

    Ethan Pond Shelter to just below Zealand Falls Hut
    Good trail quality. We stopped at Thoreau Falls for lunch (a little off the main trail) and it was very worth it. Do it! It's about a 0.2 mile detour, I think. We got to camp before dark. Finding the campsite was more difficult b/c you are stealth camping here. The campsite is just before the trail junction heading up towards Zealand Hut. You will see a big boulder on your right, and the campsite is down the hill on the left side of the trail. There are a couple of good places to set up tarps. If you have trouble finding, you can hike up to the hut and they will give you more details on how to find it. There are no bear boxes, you have to bear bag, but be cautious. The obvious tree has bark that catches ropes very effectively. We got both of our ropes and two nalgenes stuck in the tree (without our food), and had quite the adventure getting them down. We ended up hauling our food up to the hut and asking them to store it for us overnight, which they kindly did, but don't depend on it. There is running water downhill from the campsite.

    Day 3:

    Zealand Falls to Guyot
    Trail conditions good. This was a fairly hard day, due to the amount of climbing and the distance. There is also a section on Guyot that traverses a boulder field. We had breakfast at the Zealand Hut and enjoyed the view there. Hiking up to the hut is cool, take a look at Zealand Falls. There is a great view on top of Zeacliff, and it's a very short distance off the main trail. We ate lunch on top of Zealand (which is wooded and kinda lame-also off the main trail). We got into camp before dark. After dinner, we hiked over to West Bond to watch the sun set. Again, do it! It was gorgeous. If you want to summit West Bond, you should do it this evening because the next day is long. There are tent platforms and bear boxes at Guyot. Water is from a spring within the campsite.

    Day 4:

    Guyot to Franconia East Campsite
    Trail conditions good, there are a few boulder fields that you have to cross. This is a very long day, distance wise. Awesome views on the ridge! If you get up super early, and conditions are super clear, you can see a bright line on the horizon that, according to a ranger I met on a different trip, is the ocean. Even if you can't see that, you can see a very long ways. Make sure you allot time to take fun pictures on top of Bondcliff. The second half+ of the day is all downhill, and can be steep. There is a short, very steep section coming down from Bondcliff where you should have your kids turn around and downclimb. We didn't pass packs here, I don't think it would be super helpful. There is no water from Guyot until you are almost down to the lincoln woods trail, so make sure everyone has full water bottles when you leave camp. Some groups have said they had time to swim at Franconia Falls at the end of the day, we elected not to because our kids were tired and it was fairly late. There is a river crossing to get to camp, the only alternative is to hike all the way out and then back up the other side of the river (4+ miles). You cross the Pemi right above where the river/stream from Franconia comes in. Turn left just before the bridge. When we crossed, the Pemi was low enough that all of our kids crossed without any trouble. The campsite is just across the river. There are no tent platforms. There are bear boxes. There are often other TWO groups also staying in the campsite for the last night.

    Franconia East Campsite to Lincoln Woods Trailhead

    Easy peasy! Flat open trail along the river, but you will probably have wet boots from the river crossing the night before.

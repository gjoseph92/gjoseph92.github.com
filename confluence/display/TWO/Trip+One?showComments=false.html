    Drop off and pick up can be a little tricky- on the drop-off, you have to go...

    Drop off and pick up can be a little tricky- on the drop-off, you have to go pretty far down the dirt road- don't be discouraged, you get to the trail head eventually!  For pick up, keep following the signs to the town on the trail- you leave the AT.  A couple of key turns I remember- take a right at the "power anal" sign, and when you walk along the powerlines, don't miss the left back into the woods.  At the final bridge, take the lower part.  The upper bridge takes you across the river, the road, and the railroad tracks, and you have to cross back over the tracks to get to the road.

     The trip itself- We went up Goose Eye and then over to Carlo Col- don't do it!  It was absolutely beautiful, one of the best views I've had in NH, but we didn't get into Carlo Col until midnight, and our kids were exhausted (the trail can be kind of treacherous in between Goose Eye and Mt Carlo, too).  Only attempt if you know you have bomb-ass kids.

    The second day requires some pack-passing, and it can be slow going up to the top of Mt. Success. Give yourself extra time, you need to pack-pass along a few sections.  Overall, this is a long day.  But the third and fourth days are relatively easy! 

    The campsites are all great- Mascot pond is beautiful, and decently swim-able (Dream lake is not- it's anything but dreamy, more of like 2 feet of water on top of 3 feet of mud) There's a campsite at Mascot in the woods just past the beach, but we had the place to ourselves and camped out under the stars on the beach, which was wonderful.  The best water source, though, is a running stream a little ways down the trail towards town.

     The first few days of this trip are definitely a challenge for new backpackers, but it's worth it.  Gorgeous views, great hike.  And you meet a ton of cool through-hikers who are almost done and pretty excited.

    This trip really IS THE BOMB. You get to hit some awesome (3500+) peaks and do really BA stuff. There is a section where you need to pack pass on morning of day two. There are also cool mines to explore at the Mascot Pond campsite and a cool scree field to climb on day four. Goose Eye peak is amazing and Mt. Success is almost as beautiful.

    That being said, I would not recommend taking Goose Eye Trail up on Day 1. Its just too long and hard for the first day. We didn't get into camp until midnight. It was really, really beautiful, so if you have time, drop your packs and take a side hike day one, but it was too much for our kids for their first day.

    In terms of campsites the first night and second campsites have shleters, platforms, bathrooms, and bear bagging areas. As a result both sites were fairly busy. The third night had all of the amentities, but it didn't have a shelter. The three sites were awesome and all had great water sorces that were clearly marked. Dream lake (day three) wasn't so dreamy - pretty bad for swimming, but we went in anyway). Mascot Pond (day four campsite) was great, but we didn't really find a good place to camp, so we ended up just camping on the beach. There is a great place to get water about a quarter mile down the trail (towards the trailhead).

    Here are some helpful directions information:
    Getting to the trailhead: Success pond road is a lonnnng dirt road. You will pass a tree on your right decorated with boots (mile three) and a mile 5 marker (spray painted). The trailhead is at seven miles and is marked with a regular trail sign. Its farrr away though

    Getting out of the woods: The trail will eventually intersect a small dirt road. Take a right on the dirt road and then an immediate left back into the woods (trail marked by blue trailblazes). When you get to the end of that trail you will see a large lake with a sign that says something about a "Power Anal" and there will be a big red barn-looking thing in front of you. Take a right and go until you see a trail that goes off to your left. It looks like it just goes into the tall grass, but it is marked with blue trail blazes and there are big power lines above you, so you can't miss it. When you get to the end of this you will cross a bridge and see another similiar looking red barn building on your right. When you get to the next bridge (its pretty big) take the lower level, not the upper level. If you take the uppwer level you will end up on the wrong side of the road. The path splits kind of far before the bridge, but take the low road, not the high road.

    Orli T. Gottlieb says:

    2012 Leaders: Tali Lieber and Will Ross NH1: difficult first 2 days with some a...

    2012 Leaders: Tali Lieber and Will Ross

    NH1: difficult first 2 days with some awesome view, beautiful third day with water to play in, shorter fourth day, easy fifth day walk out. Meet some awesome thurhikers along the way. 
    Day 1: got to the trailhead (far down success pond road) and started hiking around 2:30pm. Just knwo that if you hike Goose Eye Trail, the trailhead is only 100 ft off of Success Pond Road on the right. When you get out of the immediate woods and hit a road, take a LEFT and the trail continues. Make sure that you fill up water whenever you see the stream flowing near the trail. 

    Goose Eye to Carlo Col is definitely a long, hard hike for the first day, but the view is totally worth it and one of the best ones we had the entire trip. We ended up nighthiking and only getting into camp around 12:30, which made for a late start the next day. 

    Night 1: Carlo Col campsite has shelters, a bearbox, and an outhouse, which made it really easy after a nighthike to get ready for bed. 

    Day 2: A few steep sections at the very beginning of the campsite. We ended up nighthiking 

    Night 2: Gentian Pond campsite has shelters, a bearbox, and outhouses! 

    Day 3: Definitely take the Dryad Falls route. Dryad falls is beautiful, but just make sure that your freshmen don't go too close to the edge because there is a steep drop off. Met up with NH 11 at Dryad which was fun. Continue on to Dream Lake to have lunch, but do not swim. 

    Night 3: Trident Col campsite has campsite to set up tarps, a bearbox, and outhouses.

    Day 4: Climb two relatively easy peaks with views of Mt. Moriah and the town of Gorham. 

    Night 4: Mascot Pond has flatground in the woods near the pond to set up tarps, but some difficult trees for bearbagging. 

    Day 5: We ended up hiking out to where NH11 got dropped off on the main road near the bridge. This may be a better pick up location then where we were supposed to get picked up because it is on the main road. Can get food in Gorham before the bus ride back. 

    --Tali

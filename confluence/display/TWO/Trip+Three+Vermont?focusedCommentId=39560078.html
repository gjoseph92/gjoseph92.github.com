    This is a fantastic trip but definitely a very challenging one. There are shelters at every campsite and it is actually difficult to find a spot to set up tarps. Day 1 is very easy and you will get to camp early. Just make sure you google earth the bus drop-off site so you know what it looks like. Day 2 is a long day and is a technically challenging hike. You will eventually hike up a ski slope (look for wild blueberries on the side of the trail!) to the summit where it is possible to camp out for the night; we kept going to sterling pond where we could swim. The first half of Day 3 is all downhill and easy. We met the other group and got a hike-in at Rt. 108. The next mile or so to Taft lodge was our most difficult of the trip and took us all afternoon. It is very steep but there is a stream along the trail so you won't run out of water on a hot day. Taft lodge has a great view and is the best of the lodges. Don't try to make it to Butler on day 3. Day 4 is the highlight because you summit Mansfield. It is a very exposed trail (amazing views!) but after the forehead it is a relatively easy hike to taylor lodge. if you walk 10 minutes past the lodge, there is a great swimming hole you can climb down to with a waterfall pouring into it. Day 5 is a quick hike and just remember to stay off the trout clubs property, they were not very nice.

    Overall, a really fun trip with great scenery and not many through-hikers. But, Days 2 and 4 could be very dangerous in bad weather. Our kids were mostly in good shape and they still had lots of trouble and they fell numerous times in perfect weather. The trails are steep and Mansfield is very exposed. The vermont trail book has a map showing the bypass trails on Mansfield. Take a copy of that map with you. Be advised though, those trails are also rough. We took the forehead bypass and it was a sheet of rock the whole way that would turn into a water slide during a storm.

    Gorgeous trip! Seconding Andrew on the trail being difficult to pick up - there are lots of snowmobiling trails (with markers) around there, and it isn't clear at the beginning which is the right one.  Make sure you follow the correct markers, I think they were white blazes, but it's been a while now.  It took Dave and I a while to pick up the right trail.  Sterling pond is nice for swimming (if a little bit mucky) - especially because it comes after a challenging day coming from Bear Hollow.  We slept on the ski slopes because the shelter was occupied, and this is a fun option! The next day, definitely drink a lot of water and fill up your bottles at the river near Rt. 108, because there was no more water for us until we summited Mansfield the next morning.  We stayed at Taft rather than Butler - it would have been very hard to get to Butler.  Plus summiting Mansfield early in the morning the next day when you're feeling fresh is awesome!

    The trip is generally challenging - we were with a pretty athletic group and were fine, but there are lots of steep elevation gains/losses throughout the trip and its going to be hard work even for an athletic crowd.  Coming down from Mansfield is very steep, but feels easy after all the tough uphill hiking.  Someone who is freaked out about heights might have a hard time with it though.

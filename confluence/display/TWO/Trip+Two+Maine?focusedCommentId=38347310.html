    Ok. This trip is AWESOME. Explore the awesome caves on day 1. They rock. Be careful not to climb up too high cause it gets dangerous-ish. Also piazza rock is cool and on a different (and short) side path.

    Despite a little bad weather, our trip rocked! There was a significant amount of mud (murk) on the entire trail, which wasn't helped by the rain we encountered, but it was nothing our group couldn't handle. Just be prepared with plenty of dry socks and maybe an extra pair of rain pants or two. We built shelters every night and met some cool thru hikers.

    1st day: We spent our first night up at Eddy Pond instead of Piazza Rock. Despite heavy discouragement from the caretaker at Piazza Rock, this was a great choice! There's a nice site about 300 feet from the pond...dry, flat, plenty of trees for tying, etc. Bear bagging was an issue (a recurrent theme throughout the trip) but really it's just squirrels and coons you gotta worry about, so we were fine.

    2nd day: We awoke on the second day and went for a day hike (just peggy and I carried packs) to Saddleback Mtn. Due to scary weather, we didn't quite make it to the summit, but it was a lovely hike! We then picked up our gear and hiked back down to the Piazza Rock site. Never made it to the caves but the rock was cool!

    3rd day: We met up with A11 at South Pond and had lunch. Great spot. This was probably our most demanding day, but it's definitely do-able. Little Swift is a great site and there were some canoes there which we used on the pond.

    4th day: Nice hike and great ending to the day! Sabbath Day Pond is nice and there's some swimming if you don't mind leeches, which we definitely saw. If you hike a little past Sabbath Day Pond, there's a great beach spot on the edge of Long Pond where we watched the sunset. It was too cold at the time, but this is probably the best swimming site we saw on the whole trip. Also, early in the morning if you sneak up on the pond you will probably see moose! We saw one, which was great!

    5th day: Easy hike to the pickup point, which is really just a road. There's no parking lot, so don't be looking for one.

    Rachael A. Wolber says:

    This trip is awesome. I think this is the better direction, too, as it's great ...

    This trip is awesome. I think this is the better direction, too, as it's great to have Saddleback earlier in the trip. We had incredible weather, but the trail was still muddy, so I would just be aware of that, especially if it's been a wet summer. But falling in the mud can be a great bonding experience! To break it down:

    1st day: Short hike, the caretaker was not too hot on us camping at Eddy Pond, so we stayed at Piazza Rock which was just fine. The caves are awesome! There are blue blazes that mark the path to the top, although some of it is a little bit tricky to climb. Probably best just to explore the lower caves. Also, since it's close to the road, a lot of thru hikers congregate here after going to town--so don't expect to use the shelter (not a problem though, plenty of platforms and tent pads) Met some awesome thru hikers here, too! Also, make sure to visit the privy ...could be a great bonding experience with your co!

    2nd day: Everyone took daypacks with layers and food to Saddleback.  This was tougher than we expected, and the top is really exposed. Be prepared for a lot of false summits, and bring layers, as it was in the 70's and sunny at camp and cold, cloudy, and windy on top. There's a little wind shelter at the summit which you can eat lunch in. Getting to Saddleback was already challenging, so we didn't attempt the Horn. On the way back, went swimming at Eddy Pond, which is very muddy and shallow, but it gets deeper toward the middle. Watch out for sharp rocks!

    3rd day: South pond is probably the best swimming site of the trip, and a great place to have your hike in. The terrain and mileage before the pond were a little difficult, but after that it's smooth sailing. There's a tent site right by the water at Little Swift--we didn't get to use it, but it looked nice. There's also a pump here, but you still have to treat the water. This campsite has a privy, too.

    4th day: Short hike, and great campsite with HUGE tent platforms. Also, don't miss the beach!! It's about .3 miles down the AT from the campsite, and one of the thru hikers we met swore it was the best view on the AT. There's a sandy beach and cool little seats, we had dinner and watched the sunset here. There are leeches in the pond, but don't be afraid of them, they are very tame. One of our girls put one in her hand and it refused to bite her. This site claimed it had a privy, but no one could ever find it...a good time to break out the shovel!

    5th day: This hike was a lot steeper than we expected, so you might have to leave a little extra time to make it to the bus on time. There is an INCREDIBLE view when you get to the road. Also, the bus can't pull off until a little farther down, so make sure you check for it.

    Summary: Swimming every day, day hike, great views, the only beach on the AT, just the right degree of difficulty, AND we saw a moose. Do this trip.

    Hilary A. Ross says:

    Heyo! Noah and I led this trip during Hurricane Irene, so all was a bit diff...

    Heyo! Noah and I led this trip during Hurricane Irene, so all was a bit different: we got dropped off at the pick-up and spent our two nights at Piazza Rock, did a day hike up to Saddleback in between the two nights. We then hiked back out to the drop-off where we got evac-ed out, and spent a night in a hotel in town (if this happens, there is good pizza down the road, check it out!). It had just started raining a few hours before we were evac-ed, so the trail out was still fine, but if there had been a lot more rain, it could've gotten iffy.

    Dayhike to Saddleback -- Noah and I each took a pack, and filled them up with pita and rain layers and water (layers are key - like Wobs said, the top is windy and cold). In hindsight, we should've made everyone carry their packs, but it was a slow morning. Wasn't raining yet, totally beautiful, but harder hiking towards the summit -- lots of rocks. Like everyone said, SO many false summits. Prepare your kiddos or they'll be sad. There's a beautiful view towards the top if it's clear out. I was so glad we had this side of the trip (oops, sorry Milas and Charlee!), because we still got to summit something in our abbreviated trip.

    Piazza Rock Campsite -- If I remember correctly, there are 2 platforms at the group camp site, and then a small shelter (the shelter was occupied, so we took the platforms). There are zero bear bagging trees. There's a little privy, you can play cribbage. And the water source is close by and good! 

    For potential future hurricane years (cross our fingers that won't happen): when we got back to Tufts, we went to Fresh Pond -- which I had forgotten has a giant chain link fence around it. So, it's pretty, but another outing might be preferred. The next day Tufts bussed us to the beach.

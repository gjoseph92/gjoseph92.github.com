    The trip was generally easy and really fun.
    The trail head was not easy to find so we just walked on the road (Route 113) until we hit the campsite.
    Speaking of the first night campsite... Don't count on the Wild River campsite. We arrived there between 5pm and 6pm and the site was full. (The site also requires a fee.)
    We continued on to the Basin Trail and then Black Angel Trail to the Blue Brook Shelter. All worked out great! Just a little hectic for the first day. The next day we took the blue angel trail and stayed at Spruce Brook Campsite, as planned. This detour made the trip a little more difficult/exciting, and certainly doable.
    Perkin's Notch Shelter on nights three and four was awesome. Watch for bears! The day hike on day four was also great.
    The hike out is easy but allot a decent amount of time for it. Make sure to continue on the trail after you see the first road. Just cross over the road and keep going until you hit the parking lot.
    Overall, a great trip. Lots of fun, pretty easy, allows time for Iron Chef and lots of sleep.
